Summary

Asurion needed to move beyond break/fix. Customers needed a reason to stay.

Asurion's connected home product had a positioning problem. Customers saw it as insurance they hoped to never use. Churn was driven by a perceived value gap—people were constantly evaluating whether they were getting $25 of value each month. The product was one-size-fits-all in a world where every home is different.

I was a founding member of the initiative to reimagine this product from scratch. What started as a multi-day workshop exploring customer pain points became a full-scale product transformation that shifted Asurion from a client-led model to a product-led model—building the platform foundation that now underpins the company's enterprise strategy.

Problem Framing

Three customer truths that changed our direction

We started with the prompt: "How would you define a joyful home enabled by technology?" The answers reframed everything. Home isn't about devices—it's where people feel joy, raise families, build memories, and find peace. Our product needed to meet people there.

01

Customers want choice

How might we deeply understand each customer, so we can help bring to life their unique definition of home? There isn't a one-size-fits-all product.

02

Customers are dynamic

How might we grow with each customer, evolving with their changing needs? How do we let customers flex up and down, rather than cancel?

03

Customers are discerning

How might we deliver personalized and continuous value to each customer, enhancing their everyday lives? They're evaluating whether they get $25 of value every month.

Approach

From workshop whiteboard to in-store pilot

We moved fast and worked iteratively—building prototypes, testing with real customers, and seeking to invalidate hypotheses so we could converge on our strongest ideas.

Phase 01

Founding workshop & opportunity mapping

As a founding member of the initiative, I led the first multi-day workshop to explore customer pain points and imagine the future of the product. We put the user at the center, surfacing insights and anecdotes that highlighted deep issues in customer value perception—rooted in real needs and ongoing technology pain points.

Phase 02

Rapid qualitative research sprints

From the workshop, we kicked off intensive research. My team and I conducted rounds of in-depth interviews and qualitative research in rapid sprints—talking with real customers and iterating incredibly quickly. We used co-creation sessions, UX testing, and survey instruments to identify product constructs that were brought to life in progressively higher fidelity.

Phase 03

Quantitative validation at scale

Once we framed the primary core needs, we partnered with the market research team to run a large-scale conjoint study (N=1,750) validating the product constructs we'd developed. This confirmed that offering tiered choices generated twice the customer interest compared to the existing one-size-fits-all product.

Phase 04

End-to-end experience design

Working as a centralized design team, we imagined the complete customer journey—from creating awareness all the way through multi-year engagement. This included onboarding, device management, tier selection, claim flows, and ongoing value delivery touchpoints. The vision was a product that earns its place in people's lives every single month.

Phase 05

In-field pilot validation

Before launch, I led in-field research in pilot markets—real-world, in-store tests to get in-person feedback in the actual channel where the product would be sold. This was a large effort coordinating sales reps, design researchers, and designers to quickly iterate on positioning and further refine the product construct we took to market.

Research & Insights

What 514 customers taught us

Across in-depth interviews, co-creation sessions, UX testing, and quantitative surveys, clear patterns emerged that directly shaped the product.

Benefits tested
Phone is the anchor. It's the primary device customers want protected, and it serves as the entry point for broader coverage.
Unlimited feels wrong. Many customers think unlimited coverage is "too good to be true" or "too much." They want to protect just the devices they deem critical.
Mix-and-match is highly desirable. The ability to choose product categories—rather than accept a rigid bundle—tested as a top value driver.
Life milestones drive tier changes. People can see themselves flexing up or down between tiers at different stages—new home, new baby, downsizing.
Willingness to pay more for fit. Customers will pay a premium to get features and benefits that actually match their needs.
Upgrades and money-back win. Of 20+ benefits tested, device upgrades and money-back-when-accident-free resonated across every customer segment.
Results & Impact

From one product to a platform

The conjoint analysis validated the product direction decisively. By offering tiered, converged choices, we doubled customer interest and laid the foundation for a fundamentally different business model.

Share of preference

Transformation

Shifting the Asurion customer experience

This initiative didn't just create a new product. It redefined how Asurion builds, sells, and delivers value to customers.

Current State Future State
One-size-fits-all product offerings → Customers flex up or down as their protection needs change. Aim to keep customers for life.
Monetization tied to a subscription product or service fee → Monetization tied to value delivery—subscription, contract, one-time service, or upgrade benefit.
Bespoke product innovation for each partner → Extensible platform that flexibly supports product customizations down to the customer level.
Customers offered a single resolution path → Customer choice of resolution path—we recommend the best options and customers make the final call.
Business and product-specific technical stacks → Streamlined technical stack supporting all products and services across every channel.
What's Next

Building on the foundation

01

Scaling to more partners

The product platform is being extended across Asurion's partner ecosystem, with customization capabilities that allow each partner to deliver a differentiated experience on a shared foundation.

02

Deepening personalization

Using the research foundation we built, the team is developing more intelligent recommendation and value delivery systems that evolve with each customer's home over time.

03

Measuring lifetime value

Success metrics are shifting from attach rate alone to long-term customer tenure and LTV—delivering value throughout the journey, not just at the point of claim.
