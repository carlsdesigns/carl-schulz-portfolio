Summary

Amazon mastered speed and price. Customers still go elsewhere for tech they care about.

Amazon dominates convenience, selection, and price, yet customers choose Best Buy and Apple for high-priced, high-consideration tech purchases. The differentiator is trust: those retailers have cornered the market on white-glove support. When something goes wrong, customers know they'll get real help.

I led the design strategy to bring Asurion's human touch to critical moments across the Amazon tech ownership experience. From a CES vision demo that set the strategic direction through competitive analysis, customer research, and a live OEM pilot, this work is shaping a five-year partnership roadmap to make Amazon the most trusted place to buy technology.

Vision Concepts

Transforming Amazon's support experience

We designed three interconnected concepts showing how Asurion support could integrate natively into the Amazon ecosystem, each building on the research findings.

Concept 01

Digital support experience

A reimagined Amazon support flow that leads with understanding the customer's problem instead of offering a return. AI triages and connects to human experts through a multimodal experience: voice, chat, camera, and screen sharing.

Key insight: When customers call Amazon support today, the first question is "Would you like to return it?" Customers want their purchase to work. Returns are the last resort.

Concept 02

Expert voice support

A dedicated voice-first experience embedded in Amazon's ecosystem. Customers connect with an expert via internet call, with the ability to switch to screen sharing, chat, or visual assistance as the session requires.

Design principle: When customers reach support, they've already tried self-service. They want to talk to a human. This experience makes that connection immediate and rich.
Concept 03

Complete Protect integration

Deep integration of Asurion's Complete Protect product into the Amazon account experience. Device badging, benefit visibility, in-store service booking, and claim paths all live natively within Amazon's product pages and order history.

Where this is headed: Asurion and Amazon have advanced many of these concepts in early 2026, expanding the partnership in significant ways. The exact details are in progress.
