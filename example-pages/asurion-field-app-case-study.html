Summary

When tech breaks, life stops. We built the product that gets it moving again.

Mobile device replacements, cracked screen repairs, new device delivery, in-home tech support, and household appliance repair all run through one platform: the Asurion Field App. Think of it as a hybrid of Amazon Prime Now with Geek Squad-level support, powered by a network of 2,000 experts with same-day, national coverage.

Since the initial concept, I was embedded on the Field App team. I led the design strategy and user experience through human-centered design practices: facilitating design sprints, product sprints, redesigns, A/B tests, user research sessions, in-field ride-alongs, mentorship of designers, and dozens of feature launches. As the platform scaled, my role evolved from hands-on design leader to domain design leader overseeing 3-5 product teams.

Customer Problem

When everyday tech breaks, life grinds to a halt

Customers need and desire fast, convenient fixes and expect services delivered with immediacy. A broken phone disrupts work, parenting, finances, and safety. A failed appliance means spoiled food or missed laundry for a family of five. The consequences of delayed resolution are real and costly.

Asurion needed a platform that could enable experts to run diverse job types while setting customer expectations through digital interfaces and proactive communications. The Field App became that platform.

Key Features

Research-driven features that shaped the platform

Field observations and expert interviews uncovered real problems that became shipped features. Here are a few examples of research-to-product impact.

Call masking

Ride-alongs revealed experts using personal devices to contact customers, with former customers calling back after visits. We prioritized call masking and post-job support routing.

Navigation redesign

As job types expanded, the app's information architecture needed restructuring. The redesigned navigation served regional managers, FSL managers, and field experts with role-appropriate views.

Daily view & communications

Experts use the app to view daily jobs, communicate with customers, and complete tasks. Functionality includes inventory scanning, sales tools, and escalation paths.

Leadership & Team

Growing empowered product design leaders

The Field App domain consisted of 3-5 product teams, each formed by a Product Manager, Product Designer, and 3-4 Engineers. A group of cross-functional domain leaders supported, coached, and mentored both the people and the strategy.

I set domain-wide design goals, facilitated strategic workshops, aligned OKRs with leadership, established processes, broadcast a vision, and motivated the team by highlighting the purpose behind the work. We evolved from a scrappy startup to enterprise-level procedures supporting millions of customer interactions.

My focus was to grow empowered product design leaders within my team and support the creativity of every team member as active contributors. When designers needed extra support, I'd run one-on-one working sessions or bring the team together for design jams. As I scaled to broader focus areas, I managed a Product Design domain leader who transitioned into leading the domain efforts.
